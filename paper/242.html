#242Denominator-Calibrated Living NMA: Registry-First Network Meta-Analysis with Sample Size Adjustment

methodsestimand: Denominator-calibrated pooled treatment ranking

156-word body (current version)

Can network meta-analysis rankings be calibrated against registry-reported enrollment denominators to detect when pooled treatment comparisons rely on disproportionately small evidence fractions? We developed a denominator-calibrated NMA framework that augments standard contrast-based network meta-analysis with registry-derived enrollment data from ClinicalTrials.gov to weight treatment comparisons by their evidence mass. The calibration applies an enrollment-adjusted information fraction to each network edge, penalising comparisons where the pooled sample represents less than 20 percent of registered enrollment for that treatment pair. Treatment rankings shifted for 3 of 12 comparisons in the test network when denominator calibration was applied, with the largest shift occurring where the published evidence covered only 8 percent of registered enrollment. Living update functionality monitors new registry entries and automatically flags when published evidence fractions change by more than 5 percentage points. Denominator calibration could alert users when NMA rankings are driven by selectively published subsets of registered evidence. The framework depends on accurate ClinicalTrials.gov enrollment reporting and cannot detect unpublished trials that were never registered.
