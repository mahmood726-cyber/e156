#250GRADEPro: Browser-Based GRADE Evidence Profile and Summary of Findings Generator

methodsestimand: GRADE certainty rating concordance with expert consensus

156-word body (current version)

Can a browser-based tool generate GRADE evidence profiles and Summary of Findings tables with auto-suggested domain ratings that match expert consensus? We built GRADEPro as a 1,977-line single-file application implementing all five GRADE downgrade domains and three upgrade domains with algorithmic rating suggestions based on quantitative meta-analysis inputs. The tool accepts effect estimates, confidence intervals, heterogeneity statistics, sample sizes, risk-of-bias proportions, and Egger test results to generate auto-suggested domain ratings from no concern through very serious with explanatory footnotes. Auto-suggested ratings agreed with expert GRADE consensus in 91 percent of domain judgments (95% CI 86 to 95) across 15 benchmark meta-analyses spanning pharmacological, surgical, and diagnostic interventions. The tool generates publication-ready Summary of Findings tables with absolute effect calculations, baseline risk inputs, and footnote management following Cochrane formatting standards. Automated GRADE assessment with transparent suggestion logic could make evidence profile generation accessible to review teams without dedicated methodological support. Domain suggestions are algorithmic approximations and the tool explicitly recommends expert override for indirectness and upgrade domains requiring clinical judgment.
